UCF SWE Outreach

"The best experience for young girls!"

UCF SWE Outreach intends to spark an interest in younger girls for engineering and other STEM majors by organizing days at UCF with interactive workshops, which are planned and executed by current engineering students. These workshops focus on various engineering disciplines, including but not limited to mechanical, civil, electrical, aerospace, computer, environmental, and industrial. These workshops allow the girls to learn engineering concepts and see them put into action through associated projects.

Not only do the girls get to interact with and ask the engineering students questions during the workshops, but there are also panels for both the students, parents, and teachers who are all encouraged to come and participate. In these panels, both engineering students and professionals will answer audience questions regarding engineering coursework, disciplines, internships, and careers.

Each year, UCF SWE hosts two Outreach events: Mystery Design for middle school girls (grades 6-8) in the Fall and SWEet College Day for high school girls (grades 9-12) in the Spring.






Mystery Design

"Designed for Middle School girls (grades 6-8)"

Mystery Design is an engineering outreach event for middle school girls held every Fall semester by SWE UCF. We invite girls from middle school throughout the counties surrounding UCF to come and participate in a day of engineering workshops, panels, and a secretive "mystery design" project that will encourage the girls to use critical thinking and problem solving to complete a design challenge.

Participants of the Mystery Design will benefit from hands-on engineering workshops and experience in a variety of disciplines. They will be able to listen to panel discussions by professional women working in the engineering field where they will be able to learn and ask questions about real-world engineering.






This event took place on November 18th, 2017. Please sign up here to receive updates on the next Mystery Design event!


This event was made possible by a Program Development Grant with the Society of Women Engineers through a generous donation from the ExxonMobil Foundation.

SWEet College Day

"Designed for High School girls (grades 9-12)"

SWEet College Day is an engineering outreach event for high school girls that UCF SWE hosts every spring semester. We organize hands-on engineering workshops, professional and collegiate panels, and campus tours in order to provide attendees of SWEet College Day a glimpse into the life of an engineer and what it takes to get there.

Participants in SWEet College Day will benefit from a hands-on engineering workshops that span a variety of engineering disciplines.They will be able to learn about different fields of engineering and listen to panel discussions by professional women and collegiate students working and studying in those fields. They will be able to learn and ask questions about college, life as an engineer, and life as a women in engineering.






This event took place on April 7th, 2018. Please sign up here to receive updates on the next SWEet College Day event!
